🗺️ Dial-a-Ride Problem With Transformers

Dec. 2020 Sep. 2021

Lausanne 🇨🇭

Research assistant under supervision of Alexander Alahi

Objectif: Optimising a path problem with Transformers. The Dial-a-Ride Problem (DaRP) is traditionally solved with Operational Research (OR) methods.

Method: Using a simulator to generate instances of the Problem, we generated a dataset in order to supervise the training of a multi type data Transformer model. The data of different types beeing processed to be understood by the model as input.

Results: On small instances I managed to train optimal solution. With more testing of data treatment techniques and architectures we could probably archive results on bigger instances and with more flexibility. The logical continuation of this work would be to aim for a fundation model that understand global path problems and constrains.

🏎️ University of California

📍 Berkeley 🇺🇸

Objectif: Training a policy for Autonomous Vehicules (AV) that would smooth out trafic congestion on the highway. Previous Research showed that with a certain pourcentage of AVs in trafic, it is possible to train a policy that reduce the perturbation created by human drivers. These works trained with data created in simulator, wich has some limitation of realism. The objective hehre is to find a method to train the policy using real world data.

Method: Using a Offline Reinforcement Learning algorithm, I could train a policy that optimised gaz consumption only with a fixed data set.

Results: The policy manages to reach a near optimal solution without any interaction with the simulator. This means that by gathering real world data, we could train a policy that better captures the real world context.

🙈 Sertis

📍 Bangkok 🇹🇭

Problematic: For a facial recognition device giving access to a sensitive area it is important to avoid any possible bypass. The main attack beeing the Presentation of the face of an authorized person via a photo or a phone.

Work: Our goal was to devolop an efficient and resilient model to detect Presentation Attacks on the device. In order to do so, we build a adequate dataset and trained a light weighted neural networks to classify images as true faces in front of the camera, or presentation attacks.

Results: The model prooved to be working flawless in real time on the tablets used in production.
